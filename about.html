Hi! Nice to meet you.

I'm Frank Wu. I love to learn, tinker, and create beautiful things.

These days, I'm building Taro Health in NYC. We're on a mission to bring affordable, high-quality healthcare to more Americans.

As a student at Yale, I encouraged others to learn how to program and build cool things. I cofounded YHack, Yale's annual 1000 person hackathon; started CodeBlue, a day-long coding bootcamp for 300-some New Haven high school students; and served as a teaching assistant for CS50, where I taught a 90-minute section, graded problem sets, and held office hours.

Along the way, I've also worked at Marriott as a management intern, at software startup Honey as an early engineer, and started a music blog that interviewed Grammy Award winning artists.

If you're working on something cool, I'd love to meet you! Shoot me an email at me@frankjwu.com or find me on Twitter at @frankjwu.
